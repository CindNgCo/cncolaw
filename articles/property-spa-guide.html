What Buyers Should Know Before Signing a Sale and Purchase Agreement

A practical overview of the key points purchasers should understand before signing a Malaysian property SPA.

Check the parties, property details and price

Before signing, confirm that the names, identity card or company numbers, property address, title details and purchase price are correct. Small mistakes can create avoidable delays later when documents are presented to the land office, bank or stamp office.

Understand the payment timeline

Most sub-sale agreements will set out the deposit, balance purchase price, completion period and any extension period. A purchaser should know when payment is due, whether bank financing is involved, and what happens if completion is delayed.

Ask about vacant possession

The agreement should say when vacant possession is to be delivered, what keys, access cards or parking stickers are included, and whether late delivery compensation applies.

Keep proper records

Keep copies of the signed agreement, payment receipts, bank letters, state consent documents and correspondence with the solicitors. Clear records make the transaction easier to track.
