Basic Clauses to Look For in a Tenancy Agreement

A simple checklist for landlords and tenants reviewing a tenancy agreement.

Rent, deposits and due date

The agreement should clearly state the monthly rental, security deposit, utility deposit, advance rental and payment due date. It should also explain how unpaid rent is handled.

Repairs and maintenance

A good tenancy agreement separates the landlord's responsibilities from the tenant's responsibilities. For example, structural repairs are usually treated differently from damage caused by the tenant's use.

Use of premises

The permitted use should be clear, especially for commercial premises. If licences or approvals are needed, the agreement should say who is responsible for applying for them.

Early termination and default

The parties should understand when the tenancy can be terminated early, what notice is required, and what happens to the deposits if either party defaults.
